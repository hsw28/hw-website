I am a Chicago-area native, now residing in Cambridge, MA with my partner and my fantastic felines. I'm a big fan of Edward Tolman, neuroethology, and basic exploratory science. When I'm not doing biology, I enjoy reading (mostly nonfiction and The New Yorker), watching movies from the 30s-50s, learning every single art and craft, thrifting, and tending to my absurd number of houseplants. I also love to travel; I studied abroad in Denmark from January to June 2011. (My favorite trip of shorter length was to the Galapagos in 2012).

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Academically, I am a fouth-year biology PhD candidate at Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT). I am participating in the Molecular and Cellular Neuroscience Graduate Program and I am in Dr. Matt Wilson's lab. I am interested in the role of the hippocampus and adjacent brain areas in decision making during spatial tasks.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

I graduated from Carnegie Mellon University in Pittsburgh, PA with a B.S. in biological sciences and a minor in biomedical engineering. While at CMU, I worked with Dr. Alison Barth where my projects ranged from studying novel anti-convulsants to trying to determine a thermotopic map in the cortex.

 

I've also worked in the zoology department of The Field Museum (the best museum!) in Dr. Rudiger Bieler's lab studying the evolution of the labial palp and gill complex in bivalves. Previously, I spent two summers in a stem cell laboratory.
